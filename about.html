About the Project

Drainage Block Alert is a project idea developed by the IT students of Barton Hill Engineering College. This project is a part of the Smart City Initiative, focusing on early and efficient detection of drainage blocks to prevent damage caused by waterlogging and flooding.

The system uses ultrasonic sensors to monitor drainage systems in real-time. When a block is detected, an alert message is sent to the respective government authorities, enabling them to take quick action and mitigate potential damage.

Project Abstract

The Drainage Block Alert System is designed to address the growing concern of urban flooding caused by blocked drainage systems. By leveraging IoT technology, the system continuously monitors the water levels in drainage channels using ultrasonic sensors. When a blockage is detected, the system sends an instant alert to the concerned authorities via SMS or email, providing the exact location of the blockage.

This project aims to reduce the response time of authorities, minimize damage to property and infrastructure, and improve the overall efficiency of urban drainage management. It is a step towards building smarter and more resilient cities.
